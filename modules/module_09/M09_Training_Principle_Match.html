Definitions
Training adaptations are specific to the type of stimulus applied; cardiovascular training improves aerobic capacity, not strength
The body must experience a demand beyond its current capacity to elicit improvement
Systematic increases in exercise demand over time to continue improvements as the body adapts
Fitness adaptations are reversed when training stimulus is removed or significantly reduced
Individuals respond differently to the same training stimulus based on genetics, age, experience, and other factors
Improvements become progressively smaller with continued training as the body approaches its genetic potential
Individuals with lower initial fitness levels often show greater absolute and relative improvements than those with high baseline fitness
The systematic approach using Frequency, Intensity, Time, Type, Volume, and Progression to design training programs
